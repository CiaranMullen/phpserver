What We Do

The 2019 Rugby World Cup is the on going ninth Rugby World Cup, the quadrennial rugby union world championship.The tournament is being held in Japan from 20 September to 2 November. This is the first time the tournament takes place in Asia and outside the traditional Tier 1 rugby nations.The opening match of the 2019 Rugby World Cup took place at Tokyo Stadium in Chōfu, Tokyo and the final match will be held at International Stadium Yokohama in Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture by World Rugby.

The cancellation of three pool matches due to Typhoon Hagibis was the first ever occasion any Rugby World Cup match had been cancelled. The decisions were made on safety grounds with considerations on the expected impact the typhoon would have. After initial uncertainty, the match between Japan and Scotland went ahead as planned. Having already overcome world number two ranked side, Ireland, the Japanese won in another upset victory, becoming the first Tier 2 rugby nation to top their pool and the first to reach the knockout stage of the tournament since Fiji in 2007. In doing so, Japan were also the first Asian team to reach the knockout stages in a Rugby World Cup. The title holders, New Zealand, lost in the semi-finals to England, who qualified for the final for a record equaling fourth time, their first World Cup Final since 2007. New Zealand beat Wales in the Bronze Final to win third place.
